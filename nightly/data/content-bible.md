# Nightly Content Bible

This is the working content pool for `nightly`. Expand it over time as new territories, words, people, and quote sources become useful. Keep the tone aligned with the owner profile: reflective, precise, melancholic, and resistant to self-help language.

## Territories

### 1. Time and mortality
- The feeling of living inside countdowns rather than calendars.
- The body noticing age before the mind agrees.
- How repetition makes a season disappear.
- The difference between delay, waiting, and postponement.
- The odd scale gap between a human life and geological time.

### 2. Memory and distortion
- Memory as editing rather than storage.
- Why remembered rooms feel larger than real ones.
- False continuity in personal identity.
- The emotional afterlife of places already gone.
- The role of smell in involuntary recall.

### 3. Attention and perception
- What boredom uncovers after stimulation fades.
- Seeing patterns that are not there and missing those that are.
- The strain of noticing too much.
- Silence as a texture rather than the absence of sound.
- The difference between focus and devotion.

### 4. Nature and decay
- Moss, rot, and the beauty of soft collapse.
- Fermentation as controlled ruin.
- Fallen wood as an ecosystem, not an ending.
- Fog changing the scale of a landscape.
- Autumn as a method of disclosure.

### 5. Cities, rooms, and thresholds
- Hallways, stations, elevators, and liminal spaces.
- The personality of a room after everyone leaves.
- Buildings designed for efficiency that become emotionally strange.
- Hotel logic versus domestic logic.
- Windows as private theatres.

### 6. Language and naming
- Words that hold moods English misses.
- Naming as a way to domesticate fear.
- Definitions that shrink a living thing.
- Translation as a beautiful loss.
- The distance between jargon and lived experience.

### 7. Systems and complexity
- Fragility hidden inside apparent order.
- Small local rules producing large emotional outcomes.
- Feedback loops in habits, cities, and relationships.
- The difference between cause and explanation.
- Why complex systems punish certainty.

### 8. Body, mind, and science
- Predictive brains and misread signals.
- Fatigue as a cognitive world-building force.
- The body keeping score without narrative.
- Neuroscience findings that feel intimate rather than clinical.
- The mismatch between subjective time and neural timing.

### 9. Art, cinema, and images
- Slow cinema as patience rather than slowness.
- Why certain films feel like climates.
- The ethics of close observation.
- Paintings that seem to remember their viewers.
- The emotional architecture of long takes.

### 10. Faith, ritual, and meaning
- Private rituals that survive after belief changes.
- The emotional need for repetition.
- Sacred language without doctrine.
- Pilgrimage without destination.
- Meaning as something assembled, not found.

## Word pool

### Latin and Romance
- `ruina` — Spanish and Latin-adjacent sense of ruin with grandeur.
- `sobremesa` — Spanish lingering at the table after a meal.
- `saudade` — Portuguese longing tinged with absence.
- `clair-obscur` — French balance of brightness and obscurity.
- `flaneur` — French urban drifting observer.

### Germanic
- `fernweh` — German longing for far-off places.
- `waldeinsamkeit` — German solitude in the forest.
- `duende` — Spanish in usage history, but useful when tied to performance and haunt.
- `selcouth` — Scots for strange or marvelous.
- `afterglow` — English/Germanic mood of lingering light or feeling.

### Celtic and Slavic
- `hiraeth` — Welsh homesickness for a place or time that may not exist.
- `cwtch` — Welsh embrace or sheltering nook.
- `toska` — Russian spiritual ache or dull anguish.
- `razbliuto` — Russian sentimental residue after love.
- `tesknota` — Czech longing with distance inside it.

### Japanese and Arabic
- `komorebi` — Japanese sunlight filtering through leaves.
- `yugen` — Japanese mysterious depth or dim profundity.
- `aware` — Japanese sensitivity to transience.
- `barzakh` — Arabic interval or threshold between states.
- `sabr` — Arabic patience with endurance inside it.

## Sensibility notes
- Prefer moods over lessons.
- Prefer concrete images over abstract conclusion.
- Let essays close on tension, uncertainty, or drift.
- Avoid startup language, productivity framing, motivational cadence, and neat summaries.
- Keep the writing literary but not inflated.
- Use science and systems thinking as texture, not authority theater.
- Let nature appear as material reality: rot, bark, wind, lichen, damp, mineral, smoke.
- Let film references feel atmospheric rather than encyclopedic.

## Maintenance notes
- Add new topics under the least-used territory.
- Remove or rotate out words once they appear in a published digest.
- Track featured people and quote sources in `digests.json` before generating the next batch.
- If the pool starts to feel repetitive, expand territories before increasing reuse frequency.
